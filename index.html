01 · About

Engineering, measured.

I am a PhD student in industrial engineering, researching how injection molding of technical polymers and additive manufacturing can be optimized through dimensional metrology — coordinate measuring machines (CMM), 3D scanning and statistical methods such as design of experiments (DoE).

My work sits at the intersection of polymer processing, precision measurement and Industry 4.0: closing the loop between how parts are made and how accurately they match their intended geometry.

This site is where I share that journey — peer-reviewed articles, conference posters, teaching materials, and (soon) open-source Python tools and interactive apps developed during my research.

02 · Research areas

What I work on

R-01

Injection molding of technical polymers

Process parameters, semi-crystalline polymers, mold design and the dimensional quality of injected parts.

R-02

Additive manufacturing (MEX/FDM)

Material extrusion of engineering thermoplastics and metrology-driven dimensional optimization of printed parts.

R-03

Dimensional metrology — CMM & 3D scanning

Coordinate measuring machines, GD&T evaluation, measurement strategy and statistical process capability.

R-04

Industry 4.0 & digital twins

Data-driven process monitoring, design of experiments (DoE) and virtual replicas of manufacturing processes.
